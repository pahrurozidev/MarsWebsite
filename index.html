Departure

We've always defined ourselves by the ability to overcome the impossible. These moments when we dare to aim higher, to break barriers, to reach for the stars, to make the unknown known. [...] But we've just forgotten that we are still pioneers. [...]

20 March 2030

The departure is scheduled at 8:00 AM. The launch pad will be at Air Force Cape Canaveral, Florida. It was the first Falcon 9 launch site (spaceX) and is still the main starting point for launches to the ISS and geostationary orbit.

It is our honor to be the first space agency to bring humans to Mars. We hope it's the beginning of a new era, an era in which the human achieves goals that were once only dreamed of.

The mission

Training

Astronauts will be forced to use all their strength during the mission. The training program will allow them to acquire skills and knowledge.

Phase 1

Earth-Mars travel

The Earth-Mars travel will last about 96 days, and in the worst case it will last 226 days. The captain will be the team manager during the voyage.

Phase 2

Colonization

Our first goal is to establish a permanent colony on the soil of Mars. This colony will remain in constant contact with the Earth.
